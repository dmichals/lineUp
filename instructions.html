Installing on your device

LineUp runs in any modern browser — no app store required. For the best experience, add it to your home screen so it opens full-screen like a native app.

LineUp includes a free 10-use trial giving you full access to every feature. After 10 uses, a one-time $14.99 payment unlocks LineUp Pro permanently on your device.

  1. 1
    Open Chrome on your Android phone or tablet
    LineUp works in any browser but Chrome gives the best GPS performance and supports the screen wake lock feature that keeps the display on during a race.
  2. 2
    Navigate to the LineUp app URL
    Open app.html in your browser. You can bookmark the page or continue to step 3 to add it to your home screen.
  3. 3
    Add to home screen
    Tap the three-dot menu in Chrome and select "Add to Home Screen". Give it the name LineUp and tap Add. It will appear as an icon on your home screen and open full-screen without the browser address bar.
  4. 4
    Allow location access
    When the app first opens it will ask permission to access your location. Tap Allow. LineUp uses your device GPS for speed, course, and start line geometry calculations. Location data never leaves your device.

iPad / iPhone users: Open in Safari, tap the Share button, then "Add to Home Screen". Safari is required for GPS access on iOS — Chrome on iPhone does not support the Geolocation API.

02
GPS and permissions

LineUp uses your device's built-in GPS to calculate speed over ground (SOG), course over ground (COG), and all start line distances. The GPS indicator in the top right shows current status.

IndicatorMeaning
SearchingGPS is acquiring satellites. Move outside with a clear view of the sky and wait 30–60 seconds.
±15mGPS locked with 15 meter accuracy. Good for open water racing.
±50m+GPS accuracy is reduced — possibly indoors or obstructed sky. Move to open deck.
No GPSLocation permission denied or GPS unavailable. Check browser permissions.

Accuracy note: Consumer GPS is typically accurate to 3–10 meters in clear conditions. Line distances under 20 meters should be treated as approximate. The burn time calculation becomes most reliable when you are more than 0.1nm from the line.

03
Setting the start line

LineUp lets you set the PIN end and RC boat end independently — in either order. You can update either end without losing the other if the race committee shifts position before the start.

  1. 1
    Sail to the PIN end
    The PIN end is the port-end buoy of the start line. Get as close as safely possible — within a boat length is ideal. Your GPS accuracy indicator will show how precise the fix is.
  2. 2
    Tap MARK PIN
    The PIN card turns green and shows your distance from it. A confirmation toast confirms the waypoint was saved. One short beep confirms.
  3. 3
    Sail to the RC BOAT end
    The RC boat end is the committee boat at the starboard end of the line. Position yourself alongside or just off the stern.
  4. 4
    Tap MARK RC
    The RC card turns green. If both ends are now set, the line becomes active and all distance calculations begin updating in real time.

Tip: You can set the RC end first then the PIN end — the order doesn't matter. If the RC boat moves its position before the start, just sail back and tap MARK RC again. The PIN waypoint is unaffected.

To clear both waypoints before the next race, tap Clear Line. Three short beeps confirm both marks are removed.

Using the timer
ButtonFunction
SyncStarts the countdown from the selected sequence (5 or 3 minutes). Press at the moment of the warning gun from the race committee.
Resync ↓Snaps the timer DOWN to the next lower whole minute. Use at any subsequent gun if the timer drifted. Can never put you ahead of the gun.
5 Min / 3 MinToggles between 5-minute and 3-minute start sequences. Tap before or after the race — resets the timer when changed.
Head to Wind ↑Point the bow directly into the wind until the sails just luff, then tap. Records your current heading as the true wind direction (TWD). Most accurate way to set wind.
Set (manual wind)Type a wind direction in degrees (0–359) and tap Set to enter true wind direction manually. Useful if the RC has posted the wind angle.
Clear WindRemoves the wind direction. App falls back to using COG for favored end calculation.
Mark PINRecords the PIN end waypoint at your current GPS position. Can be set in any order, independently of the RC end.
Mark RCRecords the RC boat end waypoint at your current GPS position. Can be set in any order, independently of the PIN end.
Clear LineRemoves both waypoints. Use between races if the line is reset.

RESYNC explained: If the timer reads 2:47 and you tap RESYNC, it snaps to 2:00 — not 3:00. It always goes to the next lower minute so you can never be accidentally over early on the line.

07
Race day procedure
  1. 1
    Open LineUp and wait for GPS lock
    Launch the app while still at the dock or mooring. GPS typically locks within 30–90 seconds on open water. The indicator shows ±Xm when ready.
  2. 2
    Select your sequence
    Tap the mode pill in the top right to toggle between 5-minute and 3-minute sequences. Most club racing uses 5 minutes.
  3. 3
    Set true wind direction
    Head the boat directly into the wind until the sails just start to luff. Tap Head to Wind. The TWD is now set and favored end calculations will be accurate. If the wind shifts significantly during the race, repeat this step.
  4. 4
    Set the start line
    Sail to the PIN end and tap Mark PIN. Sail to the RC boat and tap Mark RC. Both marks can be set in either order. If the RC moves position before your start, update just that end without losing the PIN.
  5. 5
    Sync at the warning gun
    At the 5-minute warning gun, tap SYNC immediately. If you miss the gun, tap RESYNC at the next gun to snap down to the next lower minute.
  6. 6
    Approach the line
    Watch BURN count toward GO. Match your SOG to the TARGET speed shown next to it. Inside 2 minutes the near-line panel shows PIN, RC, LINE, favored end, and bias all updating in real time.
  7. 7
    After the race
    Tap Clear Line between races if the RC has moved. Wind direction stays set until you clear it — re-run Head to Wind if conditions have changed.
08
Frequently asked questions
Why does the screen keep dimming?
LineUp requests a screen wake lock to keep the display on. If your screen still dims, go to Settings → Display → Screen Timeout and set it to a longer interval. Some battery saver modes override wake locks.
How does the free trial work?
LineUp gives you 10 free uses with full access to every feature including GPS start line geometry, burn time, target speed, true wind direction, and all Pro features. After 10 uses the Pro features lock and you'll be prompted to unlock. The countdown timer and beeps remain free forever.
How do I unlock LineUp Pro after the trial?
When the trial ends, tap Unlock LineUp Pro in the app. Tap Buy via PayPal and complete the $14.99 one-time payment. Then contact the developer (reply to your PayPal receipt or use the Feedback button) with your email address and you'll receive an unlock code. Enter your email and the code in the app to unlock permanently.
Does the unlock work on multiple devices?
The unlock is tied to your email address. One purchase unlocks one device at a time — enter your email and code on any device you want to unlock. If you get a new phone or tablet just enter the same email and code again.
What stays free after the trial?
The countdown timer, SYNC, RESYNC, the full beep schedule, SOG, COG display, and the 5 and 3 minute sequences are all free forever. The Pro features that require a purchase are GPS start line geometry, burn time, target speed to line, true wind direction, favored end, and the near-line panel.
Can I use this without internet connection?
Yes — once loaded the app works completely offline. GPS does not require internet. If you are in an area with no signal, load the app before leaving the dock.
Why is my GPS accuracy showing 50m or more?
Accuracy depends on sky view and device hardware. Ensure you are on open deck with no obstructions above. Accuracy typically improves over 1–2 minutes as more satellites are acquired. Some budget tablets have weaker GPS receivers than phones.
What is TWD and why does it matter?
TWD stands for True Wind Direction — the compass bearing the wind is blowing from. Setting it accurately is what makes the favored end calculation reliable. Without TWD set, LineUp uses your course over ground as a fallback, which works when you are sailing upwind but can be inaccurate reaching or running. Use Head to Wind for the most accurate reading.
What does the Target speed mean?
Target is the exact speed in knots you need to sail right now to reach the start line at the moment of the gun — not too early, not too late. It is calculated from your current perpendicular distance to the line divided by the time remaining. The delta below it (ease / trim / on target) tells you how your current SOG compares. Aim to keep the delta green "on target" in the final minute.
What does the Bias number mean?
Bias shows how many degrees one end of the line is favored over the other. A bias of +7° means the favored end is 7 degrees closer to directly upwind. A small bias (under 5°) means the line is nearly square and end choice matters less. A large bias (over 10°) means one end is clearly better and worth fighting for.
The favored end calculation seems off. Why?
If TWD is not set, LineUp uses COG as a proxy for wind direction. This works well when you are sailing upwind toward the line, but can be wrong at other angles. For the most accurate favored end, head the boat directly into the wind and tap Head to Wind before setting the line.
Is my location data stored or shared?
No. All GPS data is processed entirely on your device. LineUp does not send any data to a server. Your location never leaves your phone or tablet.
Can I use this on a keelboat, dinghy, or cat?
Yes — LineUp works for any sailboat racing with a standard start sequence. The burn time and line geometry calculations are class-agnostic.
Can I use this on an iPhone or iPad?
Yes — open in Safari and add to home screen. Do not use Chrome on iOS as it does not support the Geolocation API. All features including audio and GPS work in Safari on iOS 14.5 and later.
